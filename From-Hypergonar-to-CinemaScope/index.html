从Hypergonar到CinemaScope

原文网址

The French astronomer Henri Chrétien began creating anamorphic pictures in 1905. Anamorphosis is a very old catoptric curiosity, which involves distorting an image and then correcting it using an adapted mirror.

1905年，法国天文学家亨利·克雷蒂安（Henri Chrétien）开始制作变形图像。变形术（Anamorphosis）是一种古老的光学奇观，通过扭曲图像并使用特殊的镜片进行校正。

Objectif de projection Hypergonar du professeur Chrétien, From the collection of: The Cinémathèque française

“克雷蒂安教授的Hypergonar投影镜头”，来源：法国电影资料馆

On April 7, 1927, at the Paris Opera, Chrétien attended the premier of Napoléon by Abel Gance. In certain parts of the film, three projectors were used at the same time either showing different scenes in a triptych sequence, or three images joined together to form a panorama, an idea that Cinerama would reuse in 1952.

1927年4月7日，在巴黎歌剧院，克雷蒂安观看了阿贝尔·冈斯（Abel Gance）的电影《拿破仑》的首映。在影片的某些片段中，使用了三台放映机同步播放，或者展示三联画式的不同场景，或将三幅图像拼接在一起形成全景。这一创意在1952年被Cinerama系统重新采用。

Chrétien reacted quickly: on April 29, he took out a patent for a filming device capable of producing anamorphic images that could be corrected by a projection lens into a panoramic format on a screen. He even proposed a screen in the form of a Greek cross, allowing images to be projected either horizontally or vertically.

克雷蒂安迅速作出反应：4月29日，他申请了一项摄影设备专利，该设备能拍摄变形图像，并通过投影镜头校正为屏幕上的全景格式。他甚至提出一种希腊十字形的屏幕设计，可以水平或垂直投影图像。

The trademark Hypergonar (from the Greek huper, beyond, and gônia, angle) was registered on June 9, 1927. The STOP company (Société Technique d’Optique et de Photographie — Technological Optics and Photography Company) was formed in November for its manufacture.

“Hypergonar”（源自希腊语“huper”，意为“超越”，“gônia”，意为“角度”）商标于1927年6月9日注册。11月，成立了STOP公司（Société Technique d’Optique et de Photographie，技术光学与摄影公司）负责生产。

Barely a year later, on October 15, 1928, in New York, Chrétien presented the invention to Paramount. The firm seemed enthusiastic, but in the end did not follow through.

不到一年后的1928年10月15日，克雷蒂安在纽约向派拉蒙公司展示了这项发明。尽管该公司表现出浓厚兴趣，但最终并未采纳。

In France, the filmmaker Claude Autant-Lara produced a film using Hypergonar: Construire un feu (To Light a Fire), based on the book by Jack London, with tallscreen and widescreen images.

在法国，电影制片人克洛德·奥当-拉哈（Claude Autant-Lara）使用Hypergonar镜头制作了一部电影《生火》（Construire un feu），改编自杰克·伦敦的小说，结合了高屏和宽屏图像。

Construire un feu (schéma de Claude Autant-Lara), Claude Autant-Lara, 1928, From the collection of: The Cinémathèque française

《生火》（克洛德·奥当-拉哈的设计图），克洛德·奥当-拉哈，1928年，来源：法国电影资料馆

Filming was complicated, scenes in the snow presented unforeseen difficulties, but Autant-Lara managed to finish the film with the support of the Éclair laboratories. However, by the time Construire un feu was released in 1929, cinema had started producing talking pictures, and the film’s run was a failure.

拍摄过程颇为复杂，雪地场景带来了意想不到的困难，但在Éclair实验室的支持下，奥当-拉哈最终完成了电影。然而，当《生火》于1929年上映时，电影行业已进入有声片时代，这部电影的上映以失败告终。

Another pitfall: only one Parisian movie theater, the Parnasse Studio, showed the film, for which the adverts announced “Widescreen film, the cinema of tomorrow”. But following pressure from the exhibitors’ Union, which accused the cinema owner of unfair competition by using a process in his theater that was not commercially available, the film was quickly taken off the bill.

另一个问题是，全巴黎只有帕那斯影剧院（Parnasse Studio）放映了这部电影，并在广告中宣称“宽银幕电影，未来的电影”。然而，由于电影放映商协会的压力，指控影院老板利用市场上尚未普及的技术进行不正当竞争，这部电影很快被撤档。

Bernard Natan, the new director of Pathé, used Chrétien lenses to anamorphosize the optical track of 17.5 mm films, and planned to produce several films using this process. A documentary, Une visite aux merveilles de l’Exposition coloniale internationale (A Visit to the Marvels of the 1931 International Colonial Exposition), was screened in 1931 with two Hypergonars for horizontal and vertical images.

Pathé公司的新任导演贝尔纳·纳唐（Bernard Natan）利用克雷蒂安的镜头对17.5毫米胶片的光学轨道进行变形，并计划使用这一技术制作多部电影。1931年，一部纪录片《1931年国际殖民博览会奇观之旅》（Une visite aux merveilles de l’Exposition coloniale internationale）在放映时使用了两个Hypergonar镜头，分别用于横向和纵向影像。

La Tour Eiffel avec Hypergonar, From the collection of: The Cinémathèque française

使用Hypergonar拍摄的埃菲尔铁塔，来源：法国电影资料馆

Professor Chrétien presented his invention once again at the 1937 Universal Exposition at the Palace of Light, on an enormous 600 m² concave screen set up outdoors. A short film, Panoramas au fil de l’eau (Panorama Views Over the Water), by Jean Tedesco, was screened with two twin projectors and Hypergonar equipment. The joins between the images were softened by a jagged overlay. This time there were no vertical shots, but a third projector, placed in the center, allowed images to be displayed in standard format.

1937年，克雷蒂安教授在世界博览会上再次展示了他的发明，在“光之宫”（Palace of Light）户外设置了一块巨大的600平方米凹面屏幕。让·特德斯科（Jean Tedesco）导演的短片《水上的全景》（Panoramas au fil de l’eau）使用两台双投影机和Hypergonar设备进行放映。图像之间的拼接通过锯齿状叠加方式进行柔化。这次没有垂直镜头，但一台位于中央的第三台投影机可以以标准格式播放影像。

At that time, cinema was going through a serious crisis of admissions numbers. In the United States, the big production companies were looking for a way to attract the public to movie theaters: Americans went to the cinema on average 38 times a year in 1946 and only 36 times in 1952. It needed to compete with the televisions that were invading people’s homes. Cinerama, introduced in 1952, was an expensive and complicated solution. But Professor Chrétien’s process worked; the large-scale panoramic images offered a new type of spectacle.

当时，电影业正面临观众数量严重下降的危机。在美国，大型电影公司正试图吸引观众回到影院：1946年，美国人平均每年去影院38次，而到了1952年，这一数字降至36次。电影行业需要与不断进入家庭的电视竞争。1952年推出的Cinerama虽然提供了新的视觉体验，但成本高昂且技术复杂。而克雷蒂安教授的技术却证明是可行的——大规模的全景影像为观众带来了前所未有的观影体验。

Cinerama - 3 Dimensional Film At Broadway Theater, Ralph Morse, 1952-09, From the collection of: LIFE Photo Collection

百老汇剧院的三维电影Cinerama，拉尔夫·莫尔斯，1952年9月，来源：LIFE照片收藏

20th Century Fox, on the look out for new innovations, had heard about the Hypergonar. After a first meeting in Paris in December 1952 between Chrétien and Earl Sponable, the company’s technical director, a first agreement was signed on January 12, 1953 with the President of Fox, Spyros Skouras. Sponable then ordered 500 models. CinemaScope was born.

二十世纪福克斯公司（20th Century Fox）一直在寻找新的电影技术，注意到了Hypergonar系统。1952年12月，克雷蒂安与福克斯公司技术总监厄尔·斯波纳布尔（Earl Sponable）在巴黎举行了首次会谈。1953年1月12日，福克斯总裁斯皮罗斯·斯库拉斯（Spyros Skouras）与克雷蒂安签署了初步协议。随后，斯波纳布尔订购了500个镜头，CinemaScope正式诞生。

The producer Darryl Zanuck was convinced that the French system, renamed CinemaScope on January 29, 1953, would be “Fox’s savior”. The company used a few old prototypes to shoot How to Marry a Millionaire, and ordered new lenses from Bausch & Lomb (Rochester).

制片人达里尔·扎努克（Darryl Zanuck）坚信，这一法国系统（1953年1月29日更名为CinemaScope）将成为“福克斯的救世主”。公司利用一些旧型号镜头拍摄了《愿嫁金龟婿》（How to Marry a Millionaire），并向宝诗龙（Bausch & Lomb，罗切斯特公司）订购了新镜头。

Publicité pour le film CinémaScope Comment épouser un millionnaire, From the collection of: The Cinémathèque française

《愿嫁金龟婿》CinemaScope 电影广告，收藏自：法国电影资料馆

In September 1953, Fox screened the first Scope film: The Robe by Henry Koster, 35 mm Technicolor. A package was offered to exhibitors: a Scope Bausch & Lomb lens, magnetic MagOptical Stereophonic Sound, curved Mirror Screen, and film with square Fox Hole perforations. Fox recovered from its financial position, and Scope set a new standard in cinema.

1953年9月，福克斯公司上映了第一部CinemaScope电影——亨利·科斯特（Henry Koster）执导的《圣袍千秋》（The Robe），采用35毫米Technicolor彩色胶片。福克斯公司向影院提供了一整套放映方案，包括Scope Bausch & Lomb镜头、磁性MagOptical立体声、弧形镜面屏幕，以及带方形福克斯孔（Fox Hole）的胶片。福克斯由此摆脱了财务危机，而CinemaScope成为了电影行业的新标准。

In Paris, people were indignant: why was this French invention being exploited by Americans? Chrétien defended himself: he had spent 27 years of his life trying, without success, to promote his process to his compatriots. As the patents were by then in the public domain, several manufacturers produced their own lenses: Jean Placide Mauclaire (Franscope); Georges Bonnerot (Totalvision); André Fougerat (Dyaliscope). In the United States, MGM ordered the excellent quality Super Panatar prism lens from Robert Gottschalk. Other competitors followed suit: SuperScope, Delrama, Vistarama, Techniscope, TechnoVision, PanaScope, WarnerScope, SovScope (USSR), Tohoscope (Japan), etc. The ratio would sometimes change depending on the systems. CinemaScope’s was fixed for projection at 2.35:1, after having wavered between 2.55 and 2.66.

在巴黎，人们对此愤愤不平：为什么这项法国发明最终被美国人所利用？克雷蒂安为自己辩护：他已经花费了27年的时间向法国人推广他的技术，但始终未能成功。由于当时专利已进入公有领域，多个制造商开始生产自己的镜头：让-普拉西德·莫克莱尔（Jean Placide Mauclaire）推出Franscope，乔治·博纳罗（Georges Bonnerot）推出Totalvision，安德烈·富热拉（André Fougerat）推出Dyaliscope。在美国，米高梅（MGM）向罗伯特·戈特沙尔克（Robert Gottschalk）订购了高质量的Super Panatar棱镜镜头。其他竞争者也纷纷效仿，推出SuperScope、Delrama、Vistarama、Techniscope、TechnoVision、PanaScope、WarnerScope、SovScope（苏联）、Tohoscope（日本）等不同的变形宽银幕系统。各系统的投影宽高比有所不同，CinemaScope最终固定在2.35:1，此前曾在2.55:1和2.66:1之间浮动。

In 1955, Fox launched the CinemaScope 55, which used a wide 55.625 mm negative film containing high-definition anamorphosized images. 35 mm duplicates with linear reduction were taken from these negatives along with 55 mm positives for large movie theaters and drive-ins. However, very few 55 mm projectors were manufactured and Fox would only produce two films in this 55 mm format in 1956: Carousel (Henry King) and The King and I (Walter Lang).

1955年，福克斯公司推出了“CinemaScope 55”，使用55.625毫米宽幅胶片，以高分辨率记录变形图像。随后，这些母片被缩小成35毫米的拷贝，同时制作55毫米正片，以供大型影院和汽车影院放映。然而，55毫米放映机的制造数量极少，最终福克斯仅在1956年以这种格式制作了两部电影：《天上人间》（Carousel，导演：亨利·金）和《国王与我》（The King and I，导演：沃尔特·朗）。

New Wave artists such as Truffaut liked Scope as it allowed them to dazzle their audiences. Les 400 coups (The 400 Blows, 1959), shot in Dyaliscope by Henri Decae, used anamorphosis to emphasize the narrowness of Antoine Doinel’s apartment. The final scene, when the child discovers the sea, could only have been shot in Scope, according to Truffaut: “A complete view of the world with, on the right, on the left, and in the distance, all of the land, all of the sea, all of the sky”.

法国电影新浪潮电影人，如弗朗索瓦·特吕弗（François Truffaut），对CinemaScope情有独钟，因为它能让观众眼前一亮。1959年，亨利·德卡埃（Henri Decae）使用Dyaliscope镜头拍摄了《四百击》（Les 400 coups）。影片运用变形宽银幕技术突出表现了安托万·杜瓦内尔（Antoine Doinel）狭小的公寓。特吕弗认为，影片的最后一幕——孩子初次见到大海——只有通过变形镜头才能完美呈现：“一个完整的世界展现在眼前，左边、右边、远方，所有的陆地、海洋、天空尽收眼底。”
